title: "Tools Shape Thinking"
description: "How the developer tools we use influence not just our productivity but the way we approach problems."
date: 2026-01-22
tags: ["developer-tools", "workflow", "opinion"]
---

I've been paying attention to how my problem-solving approach changes depending on which tools I have open. When I'm in a terminal with just grep and vim, I think in terms of text transformations and file patterns. When I'm in a visual IDE, I think in terms of project structure and call graphs. When I'm working with an AI agent, I think in terms of high-level intent and task decomposition.

None of these modes is objectively better. They're different lenses on the same codebase. But the shift matters because the tools we reach for first determine which solutions we even consider. A developer who lives in the terminal will naturally reach for shell scripts and CLI tools. A developer who pairs with AI agents will naturally reach for delegation and orchestration. The tool becomes the thought.

The implication for tool builders: you're not just shipping features, you're shaping how people think about their work. Every UX decision — what's easy, what's hard, what's visible, what's hidden — nudges thousands of developers toward certain patterns and away from others. This is why I think developer tools are among the most leveraged things you can build. Get the affordances right and you change how an entire community solves problems.
